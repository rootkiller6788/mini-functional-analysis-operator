# Glossary of Spectral Theory

## A

**Absolutely Continuous Spectrum** (σ_ac): The part of the spectrum corresponding to the absolutely continuous part of the spectral measure. Physically, these are scattering states.

**Approximate Point Spectrum** (σ_ap): The set of λ where there exists a sequence (x_n) with ‖x_n‖ = 1 and ‖Tx_n - λx_n‖ → 0.

## C

**Compact Operator**: An operator mapping bounded sets to relatively compact sets. Its nonzero spectrum consists solely of eigenvalues of finite multiplicity.

**Continuous Functional Calculus**: The unique *-homomorphism Φ: C(σ(T)) → B(H) sending the identity function z to T.

**Continuous Spectrum** (σ_c): λ where λI - T is injective with dense range but not surjective.

**Cyclic Subspace**: The closed linear span of {p(T)x : p polynomial} for a cyclic vector x.

## D

**Discrete Spectrum** (σ_disc): The set of isolated eigenvalues of finite multiplicity.

## E

**Essential Spectrum** (σ_ess): The set of λ where λI - T is not Fredholm. Closed under compact perturbations (Weyl's theorem).

## F

**Functional Calculus**: The map f ↦ f(T) = ∫ f(λ) dE(λ) for measurable functions f.

## G

**Gelfand Formula**: r(T) = lim_{n→∞} ‖T^n‖^{1/n}, expressing the spectral radius in terms of norms.

## H

**Hahn-Hellinger Theorem**: Self-adjoint operators on separable Hilbert spaces are classified up to unitary equivalence by their spectral multiplicity function.

**Halmos Unitary Equivalence**: The classification of spectral measures by multiplicity and measure class.

## I

**Intertwining Operator**: An operator S such that ST_1 = T_2S.

## M

**Maximal Spectral Type**: A dominating measure for all spectral measures μ_x(Δ) = ⟨E(Δ)x, x⟩.

**Multiplicity Function**: m_T(λ) describing how many times the spectral type at λ is repeated.

## N

**Normal Operator**: T satisfying T*T = TT*. Admits a spectral theorem with complex-valued spectral measure.

## P

**Point Spectrum** (σ_p): The set of eigenvalues of T.

**Projection-Valued Measure (PVM)**: A map E from Borel sets to orthogonal projections satisfying measure-like axioms.

## R

**Residual Spectrum** (σ_r): λ where λI - T is injective with range not dense.

**Resolvent**: R(λ,T) = (λI - T)^{-1} for λ ∈ ρ(T).

**Resolvent Set** (ρ): The complement of the spectrum: the open set where the resolvent exists as a bounded operator.

## S

**Self-Adjoint Operator**: T = T* (for bounded operators). Its spectrum is real and it admits a real spectral measure.

**Spectral Integral**: ∫ f(λ) dE(λ), the integral of a function against a PVM.

**Spectral Mapping Theorem**: f(σ(T)) = σ(f(T)) for suitable functions f.

**Spectral Measure**: A projection-valued measure E associated with a self-adjoint operator.

**Spectral Radius**: r(T) = sup{|λ| : λ ∈ σ(T)}.

**Spectral Subspace**: E(Δ)H, the range of the spectral projection for a Borel set Δ.

**Spectrum** (σ): The set of λ ∈ ℂ where λI - T is not invertible.

**Stone's Formula**: Recovers E((a,b)) from the resolvent: E((a,b)) = lim_{ε→0} (2πi)^{-1} ∫_a^b [R(t+iε) - R(t-iε)] dt.

## U

**Unilateral Shift**: The operator S on ℓ²(ℕ) with S(e_n) = e_{n+1}. Its spectrum is the closed unit disk.

**Unitary Equivalence**: Two operators T_1, T_2 are unitarily equivalent if T_1 = U*T_2U for some unitary U.

## W

**Weyl's Criterion**: λ ∈ σ_ess(T) iff ∃ sequence (x_n) with ‖x_n‖=1, x_n ⇀ 0, ‖Tx_n - λx_n‖ → 0.

**Weyl's Law**: N(λ) = #{λ_k ≤ λ} ~ ω_d·vol(M)·λ^{d/2} / (2π)^d for the Laplacian on a compact manifold.
