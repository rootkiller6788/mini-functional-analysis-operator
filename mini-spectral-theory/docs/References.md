# References

## Classic Textbooks

1. **Reed, M. & Simon, B.** — *Methods of Modern Mathematical Physics*
   - Vol I: Functional Analysis (1972)
   - Vol II: Fourier Analysis, Self-Adjointness (1975)
   - Vol III: Scattering Theory (1979)
   - Vol IV: Analysis of Operators (1978)
   - *The canonical reference for spectral theory in mathematical physics.*

2. **Kato, T.** — *Perturbation Theory for Linear Operators* (1966)
   - *Classical text on perturbation of spectra, essential spectrum, and resolvent estimates.*

3. **Rudin, W.** — *Functional Analysis* (1973)
   - *Chapters 12-13: Spectral theory of bounded and unbounded operators.*

4. **Weidmann, J.** — *Linear Operators in Hilbert Spaces* (1980)
   - *Comprehensive treatment of spectral theory including unbounded operators.*

5. **Conway, J.B.** — *A Course in Functional Analysis* (1990)
   - *Chapter IX: Spectral theory for normal operators.*

## Spectral Theorem

6. **Halmos, P.R.** — *Introduction to Hilbert Space and the Theory of Spectral Multiplicity* (1951)
   - *Halmos's formulation of spectral multiplicity theory.*

7. **Halmos, P.R.** — *What Does the Spectral Theorem Say?* (1963)
   - *American Mathematical Monthly, Vol. 70, pp. 241-247.*
   - *Classic expository paper on the different formulations.*

8. **Arveson, W.** — *A Short Course on Spectral Theory* (2002)
   - *Modern introduction emphasizing the functional calculus perspective.*

## Von Neumann Algebras

9. **Dixmier, J.** — *Von Neumann Algebras* (1981)
   - *Chapter II: The spectral theorem and abelian von Neumann algebras.*

10. **Takesaki, M.** — *Theory of Operator Algebras I* (1979)
    - *Chapter 3: Spectral decomposition and functional calculus.*

## Spectral Geometry

11. **Berger, M., Gauduchon, P., & Mazet, E.** — *Le spectre d'une variete Riemannienne* (1971)
    - *Classic text on spectral geometry.*

12. **Girao, P. & Schmidt, K.** — *Spectral Geometry* (Encyclopedia of Mathematical Physics, 2006)

13. **Gordon, C., Webb, D., & Wolpert, S.** — *One Cannot Hear the Shape of a Drum* (1992)
    - *Bulletin of the AMS, Vol. 27, pp. 134-138.*
    - *Construction of isospectral planar domains.*

## Almost Mathieu and Anderson Localization

14. **Avila, A. & Jitomirskaya, S.** — *The Ten Martini Problem* (2009)
    - *Annals of Mathematics, Vol. 170, pp. 303-342.*
    - *Proof that the spectrum of the Almost Mathieu operator is a Cantor set.*

15. **Anderson, P.W.** — *Absence of Diffusion in Certain Random Lattices* (1958)
    - *Physical Review, Vol. 109, pp. 1492-1505.*
    - *Origin of Anderson localization.*

## Computational Spectral Theory

16. **Trefethen, L.N. & Embree, M.** — *Spectra and Pseudospectra* (2005)
    - *Definitive text on pseudospectra and non-normal spectral behavior.*

17. **Parlett, B.N.** — *The Symmetric Eigenvalue Problem* (1980)
    - *Classic reference on numerical methods for eigenvalue computation.*

18. **von Luxburg, U.** — *A Tutorial on Spectral Clustering* (2007)
    - *Statistics and Computing, Vol. 17, pp. 395-416.*

## Original Sources

19. **von Neumann, J.** — *Allgemeine Eigenwerttheorie Hermitescher Funktionaloperatoren* (1929)
    - *First rigorous proof of the spectral theorem for unbounded self-adjoint operators.*

20. **Stone, M.H.** — *Linear Transformations in Hilbert Space* (1932)
    - *Stone's theorem on one-parameter unitary groups and his formula for the spectral measure.*

21. **Weyl, H.** — *Uber die asymptotische Verteilung der Eigenwerte* (1911)
    - *Weyl's law for the asymptotic distribution of Laplacian eigenvalues.*

22. **Gelfand, I.M.** — *Normierte Ringe* (1941)
    - *Gelfand's theory of commutative Banach algebras and the spectral radius formula.*
